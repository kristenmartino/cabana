# Sailfish Ops — the Airtable console (Marie's guide)

*One page. If it takes longer than a coffee to learn, it's our fault, not yours.*

This board is your Monday morning, in one place: every job, its status, who it's
for, and where. It updates itself from the app — you don't have to chase
anything. There are only **two** things you ever change here; everything else is
just for reading.

---

## The five views (tabs along the top)

| View | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Today** | Every visit scheduled for today, earliest first. Your "what's happening now." |
| **This Week by Tech** | The whole week, grouped by which tech has it. Your Monday planning view. |
| **Needs Review** | Requests the app wasn't sure about — a person (you or Dana) needs to look. |
| **Awaiting Deposit** | Repairs waiting on the customer to pay their deposit before they're scheduled. |
| **Recently Completed** | Finished jobs, newest first. Your record of what got done. |

Switch views with the tabs; the data is the same underneath, just filtered
differently.

---

## The only two things you change

Both live on each booking's row (or on its expanded card in the Interface):

1. **Mark completed** — a checkbox. Tick it when a job is done. That's it — the
   app picks it up and moves the booking to *Completed*, and the member sees it
   as completed in their portal.
   *(You can only complete a job that was confirmed. When the app says no, the
   box clears itself — that's the system keeping the record safe, not a bug.)*

2. **Visit notes** — a text box. Anything the office should keep: "gate was
   locked, left card," "needs a new filter next visit." Type it, click away, it
   saves back to the app.

When you change either one, it takes a few seconds to sync. If the app declines
a change — say you tick "completed" on a job that isn't confirmed yet — the
booking's **status won't move**, and the checkbox clears itself. The status is
always right; the box now confirms it.

---

## Everything else is read-only

Status, customer name, address, the visit time, deposit status — those come
**from the app**, which is the single source of truth. If you edit one of them
here, the next sync will change it right back to what the app says. Nothing
breaks; your edit just won't stick. So: **read** those fields, **change** only
the two above.

Why so strict? Because the app, the customer's portal, Dana's phone, and this
board all have to agree. Letting this board overrule the app is how records
start disagreeing with each other — and then nobody knows what's true. Two
fields are safe to hand you; the rest stay locked so the whole system stays
honest.

---

## When something looks off

- **A change didn't stick** → the app declined it (e.g. you tried to complete a
  job that wasn't confirmed yet). Check the status; the box should have cleared
  itself. If it looks wrong, text Dana.
- **A job is missing** → check you're on the right view (a completed job leaves
  *Today* and shows up in *Recently Completed*).
- **Anything else** → text Dana. Nothing you do here can break the system — the
  worst case is a change that doesn't save.

That's the whole thing. Tick boxes, jot notes, read the rest.
